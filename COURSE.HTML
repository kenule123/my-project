School Of Financial Studies

Welcome to Our School of Financial Studies. We currently are offering Higher National Diploma and National Diploma for Accountancy and National Diploma for Banking and Finance.

ACCOUNTING

As an Accounting student at Captain Elechi Amadi Polytechnic, your course prepares you for practical roles in the financial world, such as bookkeeping, financial reporting, and auditing. To excel in this field, it’s essential to master both theoretical and practical accounting principles. You’ll learn about the preparation of financial statements, cost accounting, taxation, and financial management.

Captain Elechi Amadi Polytechnic offers both National Diploma (ND) and Higher National Diploma (HND) in Accountancy, your studies will likely begin with foundational courses in accounting, such as principles of financial accounting, business mathematics, economics, and introduction to finance. You'll develop critical skills in recording transactions, preparing ledgers, and drafting financial reports for various types of organizations.

Beyond academics, the use of accounting software like QuickBooks, Sage, and Tally is becoming more relevant in the industry, so taking advantage of any lab work or workshops that focus on these tools is essential. This practical knowledge is crucial to staying competitive in the accounting job market.

During your course, it’s vital to participate actively in the Students’ Industrial Work Experience Scheme (SIWES), which provides hands-on exposure to real-world accounting tasks. This experience is a stepping stone to gaining relevant skills needed for professional growth. Building a solid understanding of business law and ethics will also be beneficial in preparing for work in various sectors, including public and private finance.

BANKING AND FINANCE

As a Banking and Finance student at Captain Elechi Amadi Polytechnic, you are preparing for a career in the financial services sector, which requires a strong understanding of financial systems, markets, and institutions. Your course will cover topics such as banking operations, financial management, investment analysis, and the principles of finance. Mastering these concepts will equip you to work in banks, insurance companies, investment firms, and other financial institutions.

our studies will include courses on micro and macroeconomics, monetary policy, banking laws, and credit management. These subjects will help you understand how banks and financial institutions operate within both national and global economies. Additionally, financial accounting and business mathematics are key components of your program, as they lay the groundwork for managing financial transactions and understanding financial reports.

It's essential to gain practical experience through the Students’ Industrial Work Experience Scheme (SIWES). This provides an opportunity to work in real financial environments where you can apply the theoretical knowledge gained in class to real-world banking tasks, such as loan processing, risk management, and customer relationship management. Staying current with banking technologies and financial software is also important, as digital banking and fintech are rapidly transforming the industry.

Familiarity with systems such as core banking solutions, online transaction platforms, and financial analysis tools will make you more competitive in the job market. Additionally, keeping up with global financial trends and understanding regulatory frameworks, both locally and internationally, will position you well for roles in finance that require compliance knowledge, such as risk management and anti-money laundering (AML) positions.
